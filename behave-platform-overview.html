THE PROBLEM

Most ads are a guess.

Brands spend lakhs making an ad, then throw it at millions of people and hope it works. If it flops, they find out weeks later — after the money is gone.

Think of it like cooking for 100 people without tasting the food first. You just serve it and pray.

What if you could taste-test your ad on 20 realistic Indians before spending a single rupee?

04 — GOING DEEPER

But sliders aren't enough.

Real people don't just have "settings." They have life stories, inner conflicts, childhood memories, and people who influence them. We gave every persona a soul.

🕰️ Generational Memory

Arjun (23) grew up with Jio and Instagram. Ramesh (45) grew up with Doordarshan and Nirma ads. The same ad triggers completely different reactions based on what era shaped them.

⚔️ Inner Conflicts

"I want the iPhone but my parents say save for a house." These tensions create realistic push-and-pull. Real consumers don't have clean logic — they're conflicted. So are our personas.

👥 Influence Network

Who do they listen to? Mom? College friend? A YouTuber? Each influencer has a strength (0–1) and a domain. "Mom: 0.9 influence on food. Friend: 0.8 influence on gadgets."

🧭 Values Hierarchy

An ordered list: family first → career → status → adventure. This tells us what motivations win when they conflict.

🧩 Cognitive Biases

"Present bias" = wants it NOW. "Anchoring" = fixates on the first price they see. "Ostrich effect" = ignores EMI interest rates. Real human quirks.

Each persona also has a backstory (life story), current life context (what's happening this month), and an inner monologue style (how they think in their own voice). It's like a character bible for a movie.

06 — BS DETECTOR

The Critic: Catching fake nice responses

AI has a habit of being too agreeable. So every single evaluation runs through a second AI — the Critic — whose whole job is to ask: "Is this response genuine?"

🎭 Persona Consistency (30%)

"Did the 45-year-old pragmatist actually sound like a 45-year-old pragmatist? Or did they suddenly talk like a Gen-Z influencer?"

🤥 Sycophancy Check (25%)

"Is this persona being unrealistically positive? If a skeptic gave an ad 95/100, something smells off." This score is flipped — high sycophancy = BAD.

🇮🇳 Cultural Authenticity (25%)

"Does this response reflect how a person from Lucknow would actually think? Or is it generic 'Indian consumer' nonsense?"

🔗 Action-Reasoning Alignment (20%)

"They said they'd click 'Buy Now' but their reasoning is full of doubts? Doesn't add up. FAIL."

quality = 0.30 × consistency + 0.25 × (10 − sycophancy) + 0.25 × cultural + 0.20 × alignment
✅ Score ≥ 6.0 → Passes ❌ Score < 6.0 → Quarantined (thrown out)
It's like having a research supervisor in a focus group who watches the moderator and says "That participant was just being polite. Discard that feedback."
07 — MEMORY

They remember. Like real people.

If Priya saw a bad Nykaa ad last month, she'll be suspicious of the next one. These personas hold grudges, form preferences, and learn — just like you do.

memory score  =  recency  +  relevance  +  importance
⏰ RECENCY

"How long ago was it?"

Yesterday's experience = very strong. A month ago = almost forgotten. Memories fade by ~1% per hour, just like real recall. A 3-day-old memory has half its original strength.

🎯 RELEVANCE

"Is it related to this ad?"

Seeing a Nike ad? The persona's memory of Adidas is more relevant than their memory of a cooking oil ad. We match topics, not just keywords.

⭐ IMPORTANCE

"How memorable was it?"

A terrible customer experience (8/10 importance) sticks around much longer than a forgettable banner ad (2/10). Scored by the AI itself.

You still remember that one ad that made you cringe (high importance). But you forgot the 50 ads you scrolled past yesterday (low importance). Same system.
Score > 70 → saves a PREFERENCE Score < 30 → saves a BELIEF ("this brand isn't for me") Top-5 memories recalled per evaluation After 50+ importance points → REFLECTION (big-picture insight)
08 — THE VERDICT

You get one clear answer.

After all 20 personas react and the critic filters out the noise, a senior AI strategist looks at all the data and gives you one decision.

🚀 SCALE

"This ad is working. Spend more on it."

🔄 ITERATE

"Good idea, but fix these 3 things first."

🛑 KILL

"This doesn't work. Don't waste money."

🧪 SPLIT

"Test version A vs B with real users."

Along with the verdict, you get:

📝 Specific edit suggestions

"Change the headline from X to Y" / "Remove the celebrity shot, it reduces trust for skeptics"

👥 Strongest & weakest segment

"Young metro women loved it (avg 78). Tier-2 men over 35 hated it (avg 31). Consider targeting only metros."

📊 Confidence score (0–1)

How certain the system is. 0.9 = very sure. 0.5 = mixed signals, maybe A/B test it.

Segment report card

You get average scores for each group, a letter grade (A through F), and the full action distribution — what % scrolled past, what % wanted to buy.

"The ad got a B+ overall, but it's an A with impulse buyers and an F with skeptics." — that specific insight is what makes this useful.
09 — UNDER THE HOOD

Where does all this data go?

Every run saves everything to Databricks (a big-data platform) using a 3-layer system called the Medallion Architecture.

🥉 Bronze → 🥈 Silver → 🥇 Gold → 📊 Dashboard

🥉 Bronze — Raw dump

All 20 persona responses + the creative card get saved exactly as-is. Nothing is changed. Like keeping your original receipts.

🥈 Silver — Quality check

Validates the data: Is the backstory real? Is the reasoning at least a sentence? Broken records go to a "quarantine" table so nothing bad reaches the final numbers.

🥇 Gold — Ready to use

Aggregated scorecards, averages, distributions, segment breakdowns. This is what the dashboard reads from. Clean, fast, analytics-ready.

Bronze = raw ingredients. Silver = washed and chopped. Gold = the finished dish on the plate. The dashboard is the dining table.
